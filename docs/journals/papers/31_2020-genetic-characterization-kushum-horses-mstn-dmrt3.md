## Abstract (verbatim)

> The Kushum is a relatively new breed of horses in Kazakhstan that was established in the middle of the 20th century through a cross between mares of Kazakhstan local horses and stallions of Thoroughbred, Trotter, and Russian Don breeds to supply military horses. To reveal the genetic characteristics of this breed, we investigated haplotypes of mitochondrial DNA (mtDNA) and single-nucleotide polymorphisms of the Y chromosome, as well as genotypes of five functional genes associated with coat color, body composition, and locomotion traits. We detected 10 mtDNA haplotypes that fell into 8 of the 17 major haplogroups of horse mtDNA, indicating a unique haplotype composition with high genetic diversity. We also found two Y-chromosomal haplotypes in Kushum horses, which likely originated from Trotter and/or Don breeds. The findings regarding the mtDNA and Y-chromosomal haplotypes are concordant with the documented maternal and paternal origins of the Kushum horses. The allele frequencies of ASIP, MC1R, and MATP associated with coat color were consistent with the coat color variations of Kushum horses. The allele frequencies of MSTN associated with endurance performance and those of DMRT3 associated with gait suggested that the observed allele frequencies of these genes were the result of selective breeding for these traits. As a result of this study, we were able to obtain useful information for a better understanding of the origin and breeding history of the Kushum horse breed using molecular markers.

## Relevance to finishing-position (着順) prediction

This paper belongs to feature family **D (genetics/pedigree)**. It genotypes two performance-relevant functional genes — MSTN (myostatin, body composition/distance aptitude) and DMRT3 (doublesex and mab-3 related transcription factor 3, locomotion pattern/gait) — in Kushum horses, a breed created by crossing local Kazakhstan mares with Thoroughbred, Trotter, and Russian Don stallions. The allele frequency distributions of both genes reflect the documented selection objectives (endurance performance, gait improvement), confirming that these loci respond to breeding pressure for performance traits.

**MSTN relevance to finishing-position prediction:** In Thoroughbreds, the T allele of the MSTN intronic C/T SNP (Hill et al. 2010 PLoS One) is associated with endurance stamina and better performance over long distances, while the C allele is associated with sprint/muscle-mass phenotypes and better short-distance performance. This relationship is well-validated in Thoroughbreds and partially in other breeds. An MSTN genotype feature (TT vs. TC vs. CC) stratified by race distance would be a principled genomic predictor of finishing position for distance-appropriate races.

**DMRT3 relevance:** The nonsense mutation (A allele) of DMRT3 enables ambling gaits in horses by disrupting spinal locomotor circuits. In Thoroughbreds and flat racehorses, the wildtype C allele is nearly fixed (frequency ~1.0), as the ambling-gait phenotype is incompatible with efficient galloping. The present study found A allele at frequency 0.14 in Kushum horses (reflecting Trotter influence). This confirms DMRT3 as a real gait/locomotion gene but has minimal direct relevance to JRA/NAR flat racing where all competitive horses are expected to be CC genotype. DMRT3 genotyping would, however, be useful for detecting rare CC-deficient horses in NAR populations that include trot-influenced bloodlines.

## Background & objective

Kazakhstan is a major horse-producing country (meat, milk, herding, racing). The Kushum breed was established 1931–1976 in West Kazakhstan by crossing local mares with Thoroughbred, Trotter (Standardbred), and Russian Don stallions to produce military horses with improved body size, endurance, and gait. Post-WWII the breed shifted to milk and meat production. Total Kushum population was 4,829 as of 1980. Average withers height: stallions 159 cm, mares 154 cm. Coat colors: predominantly bay and chestnut, occasionally black.

The genetic characteristics of the Kushum — diversity, phylogeny, and functional gene allele frequencies — were not well understood. The study aimed to characterise: (1) mtDNA D-loop haplotypes for maternal lineage diversity, (2) Y-chromosomal SNP haplotypes for paternal lineage origins, (3) genotypes of five functional genes for coat color (ASIP, MC1R, MATP) and locomotion (MSTN, DMRT3).

## Materials & methods

**Subjects:** 22 Kushum horses (7 males, 15 females) sampled from local herds in Zhanibek and Kaztal regions of West Kazakhstan (2010–2019 field research by Society for Researchers on Native Livestock). Horses selected to avoid sampling of kin. Blood collected from jugular vein into EDTA vacuum tubes.

**DNA extraction:** Standard phenol-chloroform method.

**mtDNA haplotyping:**

- Target: 247 bp fragment of D-loop region (positions 15,494–15,740)
- PCR: 10 µl reaction, GoTaq DNA polymerase (Promega); 95°C 10 min initial denaturation; 35 cycles (94°C/30 s, 60°C/60 s, 72°C/30 s); 72°C 10 min final extension
- Sequencing: dideoxy method
- Analysis: DnaSP 5.10.1 (haplotype diversity, nucleotide diversity, average nucleotide differences); MEGA 7.1 NJ tree with 1,000 bootstrap replicates vs. 87 global reference haplotypes (Cieslak et al. 2010)
- Four mutational hotspot positions excluded (15585, 15597, 15604, 15650)

**Y-chromosomal SNP genotyping (males only, n=7):**

- 4 SNPs genotyped: rAX, rA, rW, rD from male-specific euchromatic Y region (Wallner et al. 2017, Felkel et al. 2018)
- Direct sequencing of PCR-amplified fragments; 10 µl reactions, GoTaq; 95°C 3 min; 35 cycles (95°C/40 s, 52–63°C/60 s, 72°C/30 s)
- Classification into 5 haplotype groups (HG1–HG5)

**Functional gene genotyping (all 22 horses):**

- ASIP: direct sequencing of PCR product (Kakoi et al. 2009 primers)
- DMRT3: direct sequencing of PCR product (Han et al. 2015 primers)
- MC1R: PCR-RFLP with TaqIα restriction enzyme (Wagner & Reissmann 2000)
- MATP: PCR-RFLP with MseI (Kakoi et al. 2009)
- MSTN: PCR-RFLP with RsaI (Polasik et al. 2015)
- Confirmatory direct sequencing of ≥2 samples per genotype for RFLP results
- Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium (HWE) chi-squared test for each locus

- HG2 (5/7 males): most likely originated from Trotter stallions (Standardbred Trotters predominantly carry HG2 per Wallner et al. 2017)

**MSTN interpretation:**

- TT genotype (n=18, 82%): associated with high endurance stamina in long-distance races (Hill et al. 2010)
- TC genotype (n=4, 18%): intermediate phenotype
- CC genotype (n=0): associated with sprint speed/muscle mass; absent in this endurance-selected breed
- T allele frequency = 0.91 (very high); consistent with selection for endurance in military horses

**DMRT3 interpretation:**

- CC genotype (n=17, 77%): normal trot/gallop gait
- CA genotype (n=4, 18%): heterozygous, may show some gait modification
- AA genotype (n=1, 5%): homozygous mutant, capable of ambling gait
- A allele frequency = 0.14: relatively high compared to most breeds (typically near 0); consistent with Trotter stallion introgression and early selection for improved gait in military horses

**Coat color gene results:**

- MATP: fixed for C allele (no dilution mutation); no buckskin/palomino/double-dilute horses
- ASIP/MC1R: wildtype alleles predominant; frequencies consistent with observed bay-dominant, chestnut-common, black-occasional coat distribution

## Discussion & interpretation

The high mtDNA haplotype diversity (haplotypic diversity 0.922, similar to primitive Central Asian breeds like Akhal-Teke and Caspian) confirms that Kushum maternal lineages derive from a broad local Kazakhstan gene pool rather than a few founder mares. This is consistent with the founding strategy of using many local mares crossed with a small number of exotic stallions.

The absence of HG3 (Thoroughbred Y haplotype) despite Thoroughbreds being one of the three founding stallion breeds is notable; the authors suggest that Trotter and Don stallions dominated paternal introgression because Don stallions were used as terminal sires in the three-way cross, and their numerical predominance in the breeding programme overwhelmed any Thoroughbred paternal contribution.

The very high T allele frequency of MSTN (0.91) confirms that endurance selection pressure operated on this locus — the breed was designed for long-distance military use, and the C allele (sprint) was effectively selected against. This is concordant with Hill et al. (2010) showing MSTN T allele predominance in stamina-selected breeds.

The DMRT3 A allele frequency (0.14) is substantially higher than in flat-racing Thoroughbreds (near 0) but lower than in pacing/ambling breeds. The authors interpret this as a trace of Trotter influence and possible early selection for smoother gaits for military riders, with relaxed selection after the breed shifted to meat/milk production.

Coat color results are internally consistent: bay predominance from E/A wildtype, chestnut from MC1R e/e, no cream dilution because MATP is fixed for C.

## Limitations

- **n = 22**: extremely small sample for population genetics inferences; allele frequency estimates have wide CIs
- **7 males for Y analysis**: very limited for Y-chromosomal inference; one additional Thoroughbred-ancestry stallion would shift HG3 frequency substantially
- **Sampling**: West Kazakhstan only (Zhanibek and Kaztal); other Kushum populations may differ
- **No racing performance data**: this is a population genetics study; no finishing-position, speed, or racing career data collected; MSTN/DMRT3 effect sizes on performance must be sourced from Thoroughbred-specific studies
- **Kushum is not a racehorse**: the breed is currently used primarily for meat/milk production; any racing use is informal/local; results do not quantify performance effects in flat-racing contexts
- **DMRT3 and flat racing**: the A allele–ambling association has been demonstrated in Nordic Trotters (Jäderkvist et al. 2014) but the effect on flat galloping speed in horses without overt ambling phenotype is unclear

## Feature-engineering notes for the model

- `mstn_genotype` — TT / TC / CC; T allele = endurance/stamina; C allele = sprint/muscle mass; source: JBBA pedigree genomic data or direct SNP genotyping if available; for JRA flat racing: CC horses expected to perform better at ≤1400 m (sprint), TT horses at ≥2000 m (distance); expected interaction with race_distance_m
  - Derivation from public data: not typically available in standard JRA race results; would require genome-wide SNP panel or targeted MSTN genotyping; can be approximated by pedigree-based principal components if genomic data unavailable
- `dmrt3_genotype` — CC / CA / AA; for JRA flat racing, expect near-fixation at CC; A allele presence would flag gait anomaly risk; primarily useful in NAR or Ban-ei populations with more diverse bloodlines; source: same as MSTN
- `mstn_distance_interaction` — `mstn_c_allele_count × (1/(race_distance_m + 1))` — sprint advantage of C allele should be larger at short distances; or categorical: `mstn_sprint_flag` = CC AND race_distance ≤ 1400
- `coat_color_from_pedigree` — not a direct performance predictor here (ASIP/MC1R/MATP are phenotypic markers only), but MATP C-allele fixation in Thoroughbreds (no cream dilution) can be used as a Thoroughbred purity flag; misregistrations or non-Thoroughbred bloodlines might show MATP heterozygosity
- **Do NOT use Kushum allele frequencies directly** as Thoroughbred effect-size estimates; the two breeds differ substantially in genetic background and selection history; use Hill et al. 2010 (PLoS One 5: e8645) for Thoroughbred-validated MSTN effect sizes
